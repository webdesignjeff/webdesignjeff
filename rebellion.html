What is this?

This map is intended to help the Imperial player in Star Wars Rebellion track the information they've recieved and locate the Rebel Base, as well as streamlining the Build Phase, one of the more time-intensive parts of the game.

Note: This map makes heavy use of color-coding. My apologies go to any color-blind viewers I may have, and hope that you can still make use of the checklist and build phase automation.

Startup Screen

First thing to do is select the starting planets for both sides. Because the five Imperial probe cards drawn are returned to the game box, the probe options for those planets are locked in the checklist.

If you don't wish to use that functionality, you can press Cancel and select the systems manually in the checklist.

In Rebellion, there are three ways to confirm a planet is not the Rebel Base:

  • Drawing the probe card for that planet.
  • Gaining loyalty over that planet.
  • Moving ground units to that planet.

Accordingly, if you mark a planet's loyalty as Subjugated or Imperial and that planet is currently marked as Unknown, the checklist will automatically flag that system as Checked.

Additionally, a system marked as Checked or Probe Card will turn green in the checklist so you can see at a glance which systems are still in question.

Special Tab

This tab contains the other functions of the map and the build phase automation.

Destroy Planet

This function is used when you play the Superlaser Online card to destroy a planet. Select the planet in the dropdown menu, then push the button. You'll have a confirmation window, and if you proceed, that planet will be erased from the map and checklist permanently.

And yes, you can blow up the Imperial capital Coruscant.

Rebels Moved

If the Rebel player moves the Rebel Base, press this button. This goes through the planet list and checks for planets with a probe status of checked that also has a loyalty of Neutral or Rebel, and resets the probe status to unknown.

The logic behind this: When the Rebels move the Rebel Base, they can't choose a planet loyal to the Imperials, or a planet with Imperial units (which also excludes any subjugated planets)

After this process completes, you'll want to re-flag any systems where you currently have units, as those would not be valid locations for the Rebels

Build Phase

This button goes through all planets and based on loyal/subjugated status, adds the appropriate entries to the table below for unit production.

The columns labelled 1-3 are which space of the build queue these units should go to.

This is why a system that can't produce units due to hostile units or sabotage should be marked as Contested.
